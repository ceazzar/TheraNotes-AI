# **Part A: About The Participant** {#part-a:-about-the-participant}

## Primary Diagnosis and Medical History  {#primary-diagnosis-and-medical-history }

Nathan is a 10-year-old boy with a formal diagnosis of Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) and Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD), identified through psychological assessment. His ADHD presentation is primarily internalised, characterised by a racing mind and difficulty sustaining attention, rather than behavioural hyperactivity. Developmentally, Nathan achieved some early milestones rapidly, walking independently by 9 months of age after pulling to stand at 6 months. He did not crawl, which may have impacted the development of his coordination and gross motor planning. Nathan also has a history of asthma, which contributes to reduced endurance and increased fatigue during physical activities.

## Social Background {#social-background}

Nathan resides with his mother, Karen, and his two older brothers, aged 13 and 15 years. His father has a history of PTSD and personality disorder, which limited his involvement in Nathan’s early years however, supervised visits have recently recommenced and are reported to be progressing positively. Nathan has supportive extended family members on his mother’s side, though their contact is infrequent. A close network of family friends and peers’ parents also provide informal support.

Nathan demonstrates a strong social motivation and has maintained friendships since kindergarten and prep, with three to four consistent peers forming his main friendship group. He is described as able to quickly initiate connections with others in playground and social settings, though he tends to gravitate towards a smaller group where he forms deeper, more secure bonds. Nathan participates in some structured activities such as swimming lessons, which he attends privately. He has participated in a variety of other activities such as soccer and tennis but stopped these as the sensory and environmental demands became overwhelming. His capacity to engage is closely linked to environmental factors with busy, noisy, or overstimulating environments making it difficult to function effectively despite his desire to participate.

Nathan was previously enrolled in mainstream schooling however, he experienced significant distress in this setting, including sensory overload, anxiety, and what has been described as autistic burnout. This led to his withdrawal from school mid-term and commencement of home schooling. Nathan’s mother Karen reports that during his time at school, Nathan often cried, disengaged from learning, self-injured by chewing his lip until it bled on one occasion, and verbalised confusion about his distress. These experiences increased his feelings of anxiety, guardedness, and hesitation to engage in unfamiliar environments.

## Informal Supports {#informal-supports}

Nathan’s primary source of daily care and emotional support is his mother, Karen, who plays an integral role in meeting his functional and psychosocial needs. She provides consistent prompting, encouragement, and direct support across self-care, education, and community participation. Karen’s best friend also provides some support to the family, although this is infrequent and supplementary rather than structured. Beyond this, Nathan’s informal supports are limited, with the bulk of responsibility for his care falling on his mother.

## Formal Supports {#formal-supports}

Nathan’s engagement with formal supports has varied. He previously accessed occupational therapy with the assistance of an allied health assistant, though this has since ceased. He has also been supported by a key worker who was employed as a school support officer, engaging with him weekly and facilitating community access. Nathan trialled music therapy, which he enjoyed, but he required frequent breaks as ongoing sessions became overwhelming. 

Psychologist support was previously accessed through his school on a monthly basis, though this is no longer available. He is now engaged with a new psychologist who works with him fortnightly using interest-based strategies such as Minecraft to explore therapy goals. The family is currently seeking a new speech pathologist to address ongoing communication and social needs.

## Home Environment {#home-environment}

Nathan lives in the family home, which has been his residence for the past nine years. The property is located on several acres and provides access to a range of outdoor equipment such as monkey bars, trampolines, bikes, and adventure play structures. Despite this, Nathan rarely engages with the equipment, preferring imaginative play such as wandering the property while talking to himself or role-playing scenarios. He also enjoys spending time interacting with the family’s animals, including dogs, cats, and chickens. 

Nathan shares a bedroom with one of his older brothers. This shared space is described as at times chaotic due to difficulties with organisation. His home environment more broadly is described as cluttered, with some broken features, which increases his sense of overwhelm. His functional performance would likely improve in a more structured and organised environment with reduced sensory and executive demands.

During his time in mainstream school, Nathan experienced significant distress in classroom environments, becoming non-verbal when anxious and presenting with severe sensory overload. These experiences left him sad, disengaged, and exhausted, often requiring significant recovery time at home. Since transitioning to home schooling, his levels of distress have reduced. The family is considering alternative schooling options for the future, including a smaller school approximately 40 minutes away which his older brothers attend, which may provide a more supportive environment.

# **Part B: Mental Health And Psychosocial Functioning** {#part-b:-mental-health-and-psychosocial-functioning}

## Emotional Regulation {#emotional-regulation}

Nathan’s emotional regulation is significantly impacted by his diagnoses of Autism Spectrum Disorder and ADHD. He demonstrates heightened anxiety in environments with excessive sensory input, particularly when exposed to noise, touch, and bright lighting. These triggers frequently lead to overwhelm, shutdowns, or avoidance of participation. His anxiety manifests both physically and behaviourally, with past examples including chewing through his lip until it bled and becoming non-verbal in the classroom. Such episodes were especially prominent during his time in mainstream schooling, where the level of stimulation and lack of individualised support led to what has been described as autistic burnout.

At home, Nathan’s emotional responses are variable. He experiences frequent frustration when tasks feel “too hard” or when he perceives himself as failing. This is compounded by comparisons with his older brothers, one of whom excels in sports and another who has developed resilience through persistence. Nathan demonstrates low self-confidence and avoids tasks if he believes he cannot succeed immediately. His fear of failure has a significant impact on his willingness to attempt new activities or persevere with challenging ones.

Despite these challenges, Nathan is beginning to demonstrate growth in his ability to regulate emotions. Recent observations suggest he is more capable of reflecting on conflict, apologising, and repairing relationships after meltdowns. Whereas previously he would hide under a blanket and disengage entirely, he is now beginning to take ownership of his role in disagreements with support from his mother to unpack the situation. This development is encouraging and highlights the importance of continued therapeutic support to strengthen resilience and coping strategies.

## Psychosocial Functioning {#psychosocial-functioning}

Nathan demonstrates a strong motivation for social connection, although his functional capacity in this area is affected by both anxiety and fatigue. He has established a core group of friends since early childhood and maintains these relationships with consistency. He is also able to form new connections quickly in informal environments, such as playgrounds, though he finds it difficult to sustain larger or more demanding social interactions.

Nathan enjoys visiting friends’ homes and attending social outings, but he is typically exhausted afterwards. It was noted that his reliance on comfort items such as soft toys (e.g., Jellycat plush) has played a role in facilitating social engagement, providing him with a bridge for initiating interactions. These items reflect both his sensory needs and his emotional reliance on structured, predictable supports to manage the complexities of social participation.

Nathan has also demonstrated empathy and care for younger children. For example, during his time at school, he was observed seeking out distressed prep students to provide comfort and support. This highlights his strengths in empathy and relational understanding when he is in a regulated state. However, he continues to experience challenges with pragmatic language, often providing extensive detail in conversations without context, which can limit his ability to communicate his needs or participate meaningfully in group discussions.

His psychosocial functioning is also influenced by his educational experiences. The distress he experienced in mainstream schooling has resulted in increased guardedness, reduced self-esteem, and hesitancy to engage in structured settings. 

## Behavioural Presentation {#behavioural-presentation}

Nathan’s behavioural presentation is generally cooperative and engaging when he is in a familiar, low-stimulation environment. He is articulate, imaginative, and highly motivated by special interests, particularly when engaged in one-on-one or small group interactions. His communication style is characterised by “info-dumping,” where he shares detailed information on topics of interest, though this can occur without sufficient context for listeners.

In moments of heightened anxiety or sensory overload, however, Nathan may become non-verbal, withdrawn, or resistant to engagement. These behaviours are not indicative of oppositionality but rather reflect his underlying regulation difficulties. At school, such episodes became frightening for Nathan himself, as he felt “trapped” by his inability to communicate. Since transitioning to home schooling, the frequency and intensity of these episodes have reduced, though they remain an area of vulnerability.

Overall, Nathan presents as a thoughtful, socially motivated child with clear strengths in empathy, imagination, and vocabulary. His challenges lie primarily in emotional regulation, persistence, pragmatic communication, and managing the demands of overstimulating environments. With consistent therapeutic intervention and supportive routines, he demonstrates the capacity to continue building resilience and confidence across social and emotional domains.

## Short Sensory Profile  {#short-sensory-profile}

The Short Sensory Profile (SSP) is a standardised caregiver questionnaire used to evaluate a child’s sensory processing patterns in everyday settings. It consists of 38 items that measure a child’s behavioural responses across seven sensory domains: Tactile Sensitivity, Taste/Smell Sensitivity, Movement Sensitivity, Under-responsive/Seeks Sensation, Auditory Filtering, Low Energy/Weak, and Visual/Auditory Sensitivity.

Lower scores indicate greater sensory processing differences, and results are categorised as Typical Performance, Probable Difference, or Definite Difference when compared to age-matched norms. The SSP assists clinicians in identifying areas of sensory hyper- or hypo-responsivity that may impact daily activities such as self-care, learning, play, and community participation, and provides a foundation for targeted occupational therapy interventions.

Nathan’s results on the Short Sensory Profile revealed the following:

**Tactile Sensitivity (25/35 – Probable/Definite)**  
Nathan is hypersensitive to touch. Clothing seams, tags, zips and paper textures provoke discomfort. This drives clothing preferences, avoidance of messy play, and distress in crowded or unexpected tactile situations.

**Taste/Smell Sensitivity (5/20 – Definite)**  
This is a very low score, indicating marked sensitivity. Nathan’s historical “white diet,” strong preferences for specific textures/flavours (e.g., sour foods, frozen foods, raw vegetables) align with this. Feeding is functional but narrow and effortful for his mother to manage.

**Movement Sensitivity (8/15 – Probable)**  
Nathan is noted to have mild moderate sensitivity to vestibular input. He demonstrates anxiety with fast or unpredictable movement and has a history of motion sickness in the car. This likely limits participation in playground activities (monkey bars, trampolines) despite good motor potential.

**Under-Responsive / Seeks Sensation (15/35 – Definite, low registration)**  
Nathan under-registers some sensory cues, requiring stronger or more frequent input to notice stimuli. This explains inconsistent responses to name, slow initiation, preference for heavy cuddles/soft toys, chewing clothing, and craving deep pressure.

**Auditory Filtering (9/30 – Definite)**  
Marked difficulty filtering background noise. In school he became overwhelmed by classroom noise, leading to distress, non-verbal episodes, and fatigue. This strongly contributes to his challenges with group learning and community outings.

**Low Energy / Weak (9/30 – Definite)**  
Significant indications of poor postural control and muscle endurance. Nathan tires quickly, has a weak core, and demonstrates slumped sitting posture. This correlates with poor persistence in physical tasks and reluctance to ride a bike or sustain exercise.

**Visual / Auditory Sensitivity (8/25 – Definite)**  
High sensitivity to visual complexity and combined sensory input. Bright lights or visually busy environments (e.g., supermarkets, classrooms) are likely to trigger anxiety and withdrawal.

These SSP findings confirm global sensory processing differences across multiple modalities, with both hypersensitivity (tactile, taste/smell, auditory, visual) and under-responsivity/low registration.  
This “mixed profile” explains Nathan’s:

* School avoidance and autistic burnout (auditory/visual overload, difficulty filtering noise).  
* Feeding challenges (taste/smell sensitivity).  
* Motor delays and fatigue (low energy/weak, movement sensitivity).  
* Reliance on comfort items and chewing (seeking deep pressure to self-regulate).

## Vinelands-3 Domain Level Parent/Caregiver {#vinelands-3-domain-level-parent/caregiver}

The Vineland-3 Domain-Level Parent/Caregiver Form was completed with Nathan’s mother to evaluate his adaptive functioning. The Vineland-3 provides norm-referenced scores (Mean \= 100, SD \= 15), with lower scores reflecting reduced functional independence across everyday skills.

Nathan’s results were as follows:

* **Communication**: Standard Score \= 75 (5th percentile)  
  Falls in the moderately low range, indicating delays in understanding and using language in daily contexts.  
  Nathan demonstrates strong vocabulary but has difficulty summarising information, maintaining conversational flow, and providing context. He requires step-by-step instructions, repetition, and visual supports to follow multi-step directions and to express needs clearly in busy or unfamiliar settings.  
* **Daily Living Skills:** Standard Score \= 68 (2nd percentile)  
  Falls in the low range, reflecting significant limitations in age-appropriate independence.  
  Nathan requires prompting for all self-care routines, assistance with hair washing, and support to initiate and sequence tasks such as dressing, grooming, and basic household chores.   
* **Socialisation**: Standard Score \= 80 (9th percentile)  
  Falls in the moderately low range, though this represents a relative strength compared with other domains.  
  Nathan seeks friendships and maintains a small, consistent peer group. He demonstrates empathy toward younger children but experiences fatigue and anxiety following social interactions and benefits from structured, low-stimulus settings.  
* **Motor Skills**: Standard Score \= 70 (2nd percentile)  
  Falls in the low range, indicating challenges with coordination, balance, and endurance.  
  Nathan presents with a shuffling gait, poor proprioceptive awareness, and weak core strength, resulting in frequent tripping and reduced participation in recreational activities.

**Maladaptive Behaviour Scales**  
As part of the Vineland-3, Nathan’s mother also completed the Maladaptive Behaviour Scales, which assess emotional and behavioural concerns that may interfere with adaptive functioning.

* **Internalising** (v-scale score \= 21\)  
  This clinically significant score indicates elevated internalising behaviours such as anxiety, worry, and emotional withdrawal. These findings are consistent with Nathan’s history of sensory overload, school-based distress, and sleep difficulties.  
* **Externalising** (v-scale score \= 17\)  
  This score falls within the average range, indicating minimal concerns with aggression, oppositionality, or behavioural dysregulation. Nathan is described as cooperative and pleasant when in familiar, well-supported environments.

Nathan’s Vineland-3 results indicate global adaptive functioning delays, with the most pronounced challenges in Daily Living Skills and Motor Skills, both within the low range. Communication is moderately low, and although Socialisation is a relative strength, it remains below age expectations. The presence of clinically significant internalising behaviours underscores the impact of anxiety and sensory sensitivities on Nathan’s daily functioning and supports the need for ongoing multidisciplinary intervention.

## Summary of Functional Impairments {#summary-of-functional-impairments}

Nathan is a 10-year-old boy with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) and Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD) who demonstrates global adaptive delays and significant sensory processing differences. 

His Vineland-3 Adaptive Behaviour Composite of 72 (3rd percentile) confirms functioning well below age expectations, with greatest limitations in Daily Living Skills (SS \= 68\) and Motor Skills (SS \= 70), and moderately low scores in Communication (SS \= 75\) and Socialisation (SS \= 80). 

The Short Sensory Profile identifies marked hypersensitivities (tactile, taste/smell, auditory, visual) alongside under-responsivity and low energy, highlighting a mixed sensory pattern that contributes to anxiety and functional dependence.

Nathan requires frequent prompting and assistance to complete self-care tasks such as showering, dressing, and grooming, and is unable to manage fastenings or multi-step domestic routines. 

Communication is characterised by strong vocabulary but poor pragmatic skills, with difficulty summarising information, following multi-step instructions, and clearly expressing needs in busy settings. Motor challenges include weak core strength, reduced endurance, and poor balance, resulting in frequent tripping, fatigue after short distances, and avoidance of playground or sporting activities.

Nathan experiences clinically significant internalising behaviours (Vineland v-scale \= 21\) with high anxiety, fear of failure, and sensory overload leading to emotional withdrawal and sleep disturbance. Although socialisation is a relative strength, he fatigues quickly after peer interactions and requires predictable, low-stimulus environments.

Overall, Nathan’s functional impairments span daily living, communication, motor skills, sensory regulation, and emotional wellbeing, resulting in high dependence on his mother for daily routines and community participation.  
These findings support the need for coordinated, multidisciplinary supports including occupational therapy, speech pathology, psychology, physiotherapy, dietetics, and regular support worker hours to build capacity, improve self-regulation, and increase independence across home, school, and community environments.

## Recommendations {#recommendations}

Based on the clinical assessment and standardised tools, the following recommendations are made to support his independence, safety, and wellbeing:

**1\. Support Worker**

Nathan currently relies heavily on his mother for all daily living tasks, home schooling supervision, and community participation. This level of dependence is limiting his opportunities for skill development and social engagement, and increases caregiver burden. Regular support worker hours are recommended to facilitate structured capacity-building activities and reduce reliance on his mother.

Recommended Use of Hours:

* Assistance with community access during off-peak times (e.g., swimming lessons, library visits, nature walks) to build confidence and reduce sensory overload.  
* Transport support for safe attendance at therapy sessions, medical appointments, and interest-based activities.  
* Support for participation in interest-based groups (e.g., LEGO clubs, science activities) to enhance social interaction and resilience.

Regular support worker involvement will promote Nathan’s independence, improve social participation, and reduce long-term reliance on family.

**2\. Occupational Therapy**

Nathan requires ongoing occupational therapy to address sensory processing challenges, executive functioning deficits, and delays in fine and gross motor coordination.

Recommended Areas of Focus:

* Development of self-care routines (dressing, grooming, shower sequencing) using visual schedules and task analysis.  
* Sensory regulation strategies including a personalised sensory diet, proprioceptive “heavy-work” activities, and environmental modifications.   
* Executive functioning support such as visual checklists, timers, and graded tasks to improve initiation, organisation, and persistence.  
* Home environment organisation strategies to reduce clutter and sensory overload.

Regular OT sessions will build Nathan’s capacity to manage daily living tasks, reduce anxiety, and increase participation across home and community settings.

**3\. Speech Pathology**

Nathan demonstrates strong vocabulary but ongoing pragmatic language difficulties, impacting his ability to communicate needs and maintain peer relationships.

Recommended Areas of Focus:

* Social communication skills including conversational turn-taking, topic maintenance, and interpreting non-verbal cues.  
* Functional communication strategies to enable Nathan to request breaks or support in high-sensory situations.  
* Support for comprehension and summarising to improve classroom and community participation.

Speech pathology intervention will improve Nathan’s confidence, social engagement, and capacity to advocate for his needs.

**4\. Psychology**

Nathan presents with significant anxiety, low confidence, and fear of failure, which limit his engagement in new or challenging tasks.

Recommended Areas of Focus:

* Anxiety management and coping strategies to reduce distress in unfamiliar or overstimulating environments.  
* Emotional regulation and resilience training to build confidence when attempting new tasks.  
* Gradual exposure and confidence-building to support transition back into structured learning environments.

Regular psychological support will enhance Nathan’s emotional wellbeing and assist him to manage sensory and social challenges.

**5\. Physiotherapy**

Nathan demonstrates weak core strength, reduced endurance, and poor balance, contributing to frequent tripping and fatigue.

Recommended Areas of Focus:

* Core strengthening and postural control exercises to improve endurance for school and community activities.  
* Balance and coordination training to reduce falls risk and improve confidence with physical play.   
* Graded aerobic activity to build cardiovascular fitness and reduce fatigue.

Physiotherapy input will improve Nathan’s physical capacity, enabling greater participation in community and recreational activities.

**6\. Dietitian**

Nathan’s strong taste and smell sensitivities contribute to a restricted diet and limited tolerance of food textures.

Recommended Areas of Focus:

* Gradual food chaining to safely expand his diet while respecting sensory preferences.  
* Education for his mother on nutritional planning and strategies for introducing new foods.  
* Monitoring of growth and nutritional adequacy.

Dietetic support will improve nutritional intake and reduce the stress associated with mealtimes.

**7\. Consumables / Assistive Equipment**

Nathan requires a range of sensory tools to support regulation and independence across environments.

Recommended Funding:

* Noise-cancelling headphones for auditory filtering.  
* Weighted lap pads or blankets, chewable sensory tools, and seamless clothing to reduce tactile discomfort.  
* Visual supports such as whiteboards, laminated checklists, and an iPad or Kindle to avoid tactile triggers from paper.

Provision of these consumables will assist Nathan to maintain comfort, improve self-regulation, and participate more fully in learning and community activities.
