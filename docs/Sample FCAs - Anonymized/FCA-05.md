# **Report Overview** {#report-overview}

This report has been prepared based on the information available at the time of assessment, consultation, intervention, and clinical analysis of the client’s goals, circumstances, and needs. While the information is accurate at the time of writing, it should be recognised that many factors can influence a person’s function and performance, and further assessment may be required in the future.

Occupational Therapy Melbourne acknowledges that some aspects of this report may highlight areas of functional difficulty, which may feel confronting to clients and their support. The intention of this report is not to focus on deficits, but rather to identify opportunities for support and recommend strategies and resources that enable the client to live as independently, safely, and meaningfully as possible.

This report is prepared for the named client and relevant stakeholders for the purpose of supporting service delivery, funding, and planning. It contains confidential and privileged information and should be used only for its intended purpose.

# **Part A: About The Participant** {#part-a:-about-the-participant}

## Primary Diagnosis and Medical History  {#primary-diagnosis-and-medical-history }

Ethan has a confirmed diagnosis of Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) and Moderate Intellectual Disability, both identified in 2025 following formal assessment. These conditions impact her ability to navigate social environments, manage changes in routine, and maintain independence in some community and domestic domains.

Autism Spectrum Disorder is characterised by differences in communication, social interaction, and flexibility of thought, often resulting in challenges with adapting to new or unfamiliar situations. In Ethan’s case, these challenges are primarily seen in her preference for one-on-one interactions, avoidance of larger social groups, and reliance on familiar environments for confidence and comfort.

Moderate Intellectual Disability is associated with limitations in adaptive functioning across conceptual, social, and practical domains. For Ethan, this presents as reduced problem-solving skills, reliance on her father for shared tasks, and difficulties with community mobility and transitions such as moving to independent housing. Despite these challenges, Ethan demonstrates strong functional skills in self-care and daily routines when supported by structure and familiar strategies.

## Social Background {#social-background}

Ethan has lived in the family home in Hallam since 1974\. This home has been a source of stability throughout her life and is shared with her father, who is her primary carer and support. Ethan was visibly emotional when discussing her mother’s passing, reflecting the strong family connections and support structures that have underpinned her daily life.

Despite this longstanding living arrangement, Ethan has expressed a clear and consistent goal of moving into her own accommodation. She would like to live closer to the beach, ideally in the Highett or Bentleigh areas, as she is familiar with and comfortable in these environments. This desire reflects both a wish for greater independence and a strong personal connection to nature and coastal settings, which she describes as calming and enjoyable.

Ethan identifies as a social individual within small circles but does not currently engage in structured community or social groups. She is satisfied with her existing friendships and maintains regular contact with her family, which form the foundation of her social and emotional supports.

* **Father:** Lives with Ethan and provides daily emotional and practical support, including shared responsibility for household and community tasks.  
* **Brother (Michael):** Lives in Alexandra, approximately 170km away. Although they only see each other 4–5 times per year, they maintain weekly phone contact, providing ongoing emotional connection.  
* **Friend (Avril):** Long-term friend since 2012, originally met during hospital volunteer work while Ethan’s mother was being treated for cancer. Avril remains a supportive presence in Ethan’s life, alongside her father.

## WHODAS 2.0 {#whodas-2.0}

The adult version of the WHODAS 2.0 is a 36-item measure that assesses disability in adults aged 18 years and older. It assesses disability across six domains which include understanding and communicating, getting around, self-care, getting along with people, life activities and participation in society. The WHODAS 2.0 is considered a best-practice assessment for individuals with a primary disability according to the NDIS.

The outcome of the WHODAS 2.0 provides a percentage of impairment, based on the results of the questions answered, with 100% indicating the highest level of identified disability.

Ethan’s results were as follows:

* **Understanding and Communicating:** 11/30 (37% disability) which identifies mild to moderate difficulty processing and communicating information. She manages well in familiar routines but struggles with complex instructions or problem-solving in unfamiliar situations.  
* **Getting Around:** 13/25 (52% disability) which highlights reduced balance and mobility confidence, particularly on stairs and uneven terrain. She avoids buses due to accessibility barriers but is confident with trains.  
* **Self-Care:** 5/20 (25% disability) this indicates strong independence in showering, dressing, grooming, and toileting, with minimal difficulty and safety strategies in place.  
* **Getting Along with People:** 11/25 (44% disability) this reflects challenges with social engagement, particularly in group or unfamiliar contexts. Ethan prefers one-on-one interactions and avoids large gatherings.  
* **Life Activities:** 21/40 (52% disability) which demonstrates partial independence, with shared responsibility for cooking, cleaning, and laundry. Relies on her father for more complex household tasks.  
* **Participation in Society:** 27/40 (68% disability) which is her most impaired domain, reflecting limited community engagement (leaves home about once weekly) and reliance on her father for outings. Transport barriers and reduced confidence further restrict her participation.

The above assessment was completed by Ethan, who scored 88 out of 180 (49%) overall on the WHODAS 2.0, which would rate Ethan as having moderate functional difficulties with participation in daily activities due to her disability. This assessment indicates that Ethan’s most significant barriers are identified in community participation, life activities, and mobility.

## Vineland-3 Domain Level  {#vineland-3-domain-level}

The Vineland Adaptive Behavior Scales, Third Edition (Vineland-3) Domain-Level Interview was completed with Ethan to evaluate her adaptive functioning. The Vineland-3 provides norm-referenced scores (Mean \= 100, SD \= 15), with lower scores reflecting reduced functional independence across everyday skills.

Ethan’s results were as follows:

* **Communication:** Standard Score \= 69 (2nd percentile)  
  Falls in the low range, indicating significant difficulty with communication skills. Ethan is able to express basic needs in familiar contexts but struggles with complex instructions, abstract concepts, or unfamiliar environments. She benefits from step-by-step guidance, repetition, and visual or verbal prompts.

* **Daily Living Skills:** Standard Score \= 81 (10th percentile)  
  Falls in the moderately low range, suggesting reduced independence in daily routines. Ethan is independent in self-care (e.g., showering, dressing, grooming) but shares domestic responsibilities such as cooking, cleaning, and laundry with her father. She has limited capacity to manage complex or novel household tasks without support.

* **Socialisation:** Standard Score \= 48 (\<1st percentile)  
  Falls in the extremely low range, reflecting very limited social adaptive functioning. Ethan prefers one-on-one interactions, avoids group settings, and experiences discomfort in unfamiliar social contexts. While she maintains strong relationships with her father, brother, and long-term friend Avril, she does not engage in structured social or community groups.

* **Motor Skills:** Standard Score \= 33 (\<1st percentile)  
  Falls in the extremely low range, reflecting significant challenges with balance and coordination. Although she manages transfers independently, Ethan experiences reduced stability when navigating steps, uneven terrain, or crowded environments. These difficulties increase her risk of falls and limit her confidence in community mobility.

## Maladaptive Behaviour Scales {#maladaptive-behaviour-scales}

As part of the Vineland-3, Ethan also completed the Maladaptive Behaviour Scales, which assess emotional and behavioural concerns that may interfere with adaptive functioning.

* **Internalising** (Raw Score \= 13\)  
  This elevated score suggests the presence of internalising behaviours such as anxiety, sadness, worry, or withdrawal. For Ethan, this is consistent with her avoidance of group social settings, reduced confidence in unfamiliar environments, and episodes of sadness when reflecting on her mother’s passing. These behaviours may limit her participation even when she possesses the necessary skills.

* **Externalising** (Raw Score \= 2\)  
  This low score suggests minimal externalising behaviours such as aggression, oppositionality, or hyperactivity. Ethan does not present with behavioural dysregulation and is described as cooperative, calm, and pleasant in her interactions.

Ethan’s Vineland-3 results indicate global adaptive functioning limitations, with her most pronounced challenges in socialisation and motor skills (both \<1st percentile). Communication and daily living skills also fall below age expectations, reflecting reduced independence in complex tasks.

The Maladaptive Behaviour results highlight a pattern of internalising difficulties (anxiety, withdrawal) without externalising concerns. This profile is consistent with her diagnoses of autism and moderate intellectual disability, and aligns with observed functional challenges including limited social participation, reliance on family supports, and reduced confidence in community mobility.

## Summary of Functional Impairments {#summary-of-functional-impairments}

Ethan is a 54-year-old woman with a diagnosis of Autism Spectrum Disorder and Moderate Intellectual Disability, which together have a significant impact on her adaptive functioning across multiple life domains. While she demonstrates independence in personal care tasks such as showering, dressing, grooming, and toileting, her intellectual disability and autism contribute to limitations in communication, problem-solving, and social engagement. Standardised assessments confirmed these challenges, with Vineland-3 results falling well below the normative range across domains: Communication (2nd percentile), Daily Living Skills (10th percentile), Socialisation (\<1st percentile), and Motor Skills (\<1st percentile). These findings highlight Ethan’s strengths in self-care routines but significant difficulties in socialisation, balance, and independent domestic management.

Her WHODAS 2.0 results (overall 49% disability) reinforce these limitations, showing the greatest functional impairments in participation in society (68%), life activities (52%), and mobility (52%). While Ethan is able to contribute to household tasks and attend weekly shopping with her father, she lacks the capacity to manage these activities independently. She remains heavily reliant on her father for shared domestic tasks, transport, and community participation. Reduced balance and motor skills restrict her mobility, particularly on stairs and uneven terrain, further limiting her confidence in accessing the community safely.

Socially, Ethan demonstrates very low adaptive functioning. She avoids group settings, prefers one-on-one interactions, and maintains a very small support network consisting of her father, her brother (remote support), and a close friend. This reduced social participation contributes to isolation and restricts her engagement in meaningful community activities.

Finally, maladaptive behaviour results highlight elevated internalising behaviours (e.g., sadness, withdrawal, and anxiety), which further impact her capacity to engage in new or challenging situations. In contrast, she demonstrates very low externalising behaviours, presenting as calm, cooperative, and pleasant, with no behavioural dysregulation.

Overall, Ethan’s functional impairments are most evident in the areas of community access, social participation, domestic independence, and mobility, while her relative strengths are in self-care and financial management. These findings highlight the need for ongoing supports across allied health, consumables, and capacity building to promote independence, safety, and quality of life.

## Recommendations {#recommendations}

Based on the clinical assessment and standardised tools, the following recommendations are made to support  independence, safety, and wellbeing:

**1\. Support Worker**  
Ethan does not currently access formal support worker hours and relies heavily on her father for community access and shared household tasks. This arrangement is insufficient to meet her functional needs, particularly considering her reduced balance, avoidance of group social situations, and reliance on family for daily living and participation. Ethan would benefit from regular support worker hours to build independence, improve social participation, and reduce long-term reliance on her father.

Recommended Use of Hours:

* Assistance with community access, including support to attend leisure and recreational activities (e.g., swimming, bowling, beach and nature walks) to reduce isolation and increase meaningful participation.  
* Transport support for safe access to shops, medical appointments, and community activities, particularly where barriers exist with buses and public accessibility.  
* Skill development in cooking, cleaning, laundry, and shopping to increase independence and prepare for transition to independent living.  
* Support to gradually engage in social and community groups in line with Ethan’s goals, confidence, and comfort level.

Increased support worker involvement would facilitate Ethan’s engagement in meaningful activities, improve functional independence, and reduce reliance on her father as her primary support.

**2\. Occupational Therapy**   
Ethan requires ongoing occupational therapy to develop the skills and strategies necessary for independent living. She currently shares household tasks with her father and has limited capacity to manage these independently. In addition, Ethan has expressed a clear goal of moving into a rental property closer to the beach, which will require structured planning and transition support.

Recommended Areas of Focus:

* Independent living skills training (meal preparation, household organisation, laundry routines).  
* Environmental modifications and strategies to improve safety at home and in future accommodation (e.g., grab rails, non-slip mats).  
* Support with transition planning for independent housing, including rental applications, move logistics, and establishing routines in a new environment.  
* Gradual capacity-building in community participation tasks, including independent shopping and recreational activities.

Regular OT intervention would increase Ethan’s independence, improve safety, and support her transition to her goal of independent living.

**3\. Physiotherapy**  
Ethan demonstrates significant motor skill deficits and balance difficulties, increasing her risk of falls, particularly on stairs and uneven terrain. She currently avoids certain community settings due to reduced confidence with mobility.

Recommended Areas of Focus:

* Balance and coordination training to improve mobility safety.  
* Fall prevention strategies, including techniques for navigating stairs and uneven surfaces.  
* Strength and endurance training to build resilience for community and daily activities.  
* Confidence-building to support safe and independent community access.

Regular physiotherapy would reduce Ethan’s falls risk, increase safety, and improve her confidence to engage in community participation.

**4\. Psychology**  
Ethan demonstrates elevated internalising behaviours (e.g., sadness, withdrawal, anxiety) which impact her confidence and willingness to participate in community or group activities. She currently attends psychology sessions every 10 weeks, which is beneficial but insufficient given her ongoing needs.

Recommended Areas of Focus:

* Strategies to manage grief related to her mother’s passing and ongoing anxiety.  
* Emotional regulation support to build resilience and confidence in unfamiliar situations.  
* Therapy to gradually increase social confidence and reduce avoidance of group settings.  
* Adjustment support during her planned transition to independent living.

Increased psychology input would strengthen Ethan’s coping strategies, improve social confidence, and reduce the impact of internalising behaviours on her daily functioning.

**5\. Podiatry**  
Ethan experiences toenail fungal infection which impacts her foot comfort and mobility. Untreated foot conditions may exacerbate balance issues and increase falls risk.

Recommended Areas of Focus:

* Ongoing podiatry treatment to maintain foot health.  
* Preventative management to reduce pain, improve stability, and support safe mobility.

Regular podiatry care would support Ethan’s overall mobility safety and wellbeing.

**6\. Consumables**  
Ethan requires daily use of incontinence products to maintain comfort, independence, and dignity. She reports that incontinence pants are more effective than pads in meeting her needs.

Recommended Funding:

* Ongoing supply of Comfort First incontinence pants for daily use.

Provision of consumables will allow Ethan to maintain independence and reduce the risk of social withdrawal due to continence concerns.

**7\. Support Coordination**  
Ethan’s functional profile and goals highlight the need for ongoing support coordination to assist in navigating her plan and reducing reliance on her father.

Recommended Areas of Focus:

* Implementation of funded supports and assistance with linking to allied health providers (OT, physiotherapy, psychology, podiatry).  
* Support with the transition into independent rental accommodation, including applications, liaising with housing providers, and setting up essential services.  
* Assistance to identify and connect with appropriate social and recreational opportunities.  
* Guidance with plan navigation and monitoring to ensure Ethan’s supports remain adequate and responsive to her needs.

Support coordination will ensure Ethan’s plan is effectively implemented and that her transition to greater independence is supported in a structured and sustainable way.
