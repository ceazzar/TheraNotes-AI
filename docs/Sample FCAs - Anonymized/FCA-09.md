# **Part A: About The Participant** {#part-a:-about-the-participant}

## Primary Diagnosis and Medical History  {#primary-diagnosis-and-medical-history }

Liam is an adult male with a primary diagnosis of Schizoaffective Disorder, diagnosed approximately ten years ago. He also experiences longstanding anxiety and depression, which interact with his primary diagnosis and contribute to persistent psychosocial disability. Liam has been an NDIS participant for approximately five years and requires ongoing supports to manage the functional impact of his mental health conditions on daily living and community participation.

Schizoaffective Disorder is a chronic mental health condition that affects Liam’s mood regulation, cognitive processing, motivation and capacity to consistently engage in daily life. In Liam’s case, the condition is not episodic in nature but rather, it presents as an enduring disability with ongoing functional consequences, even during periods of relative mental health stability. Liam experiences fluctuations in energy, concentration and emotional resilience, which significantly limit his ability to initiate, organise and sustain everyday activities without external support.

Liam experiences persistent difficulties with task initiation, memory, concentration and executive functioning, which impact his capacity to manage personal care routines, household tasks, appointments and community access independently. These cognitive and motivational challenges are compounded by chronic fatigue and longstanding sleep disturbance, further reducing Liam’s functional stamina and consistency across the day.

Anxiety is a significant contributing factor to Liam’s functional presentation. He experiences heightened stress in busy, noisy or unpredictable environments and demonstrates a strong preference for quiet, low-stimulation settings. Anxiety contributes to avoidance of crowded places, withdrawal from community environments and reliance on familiar routines to maintain emotional regulation. As a result, Liam’s participation in community and social activities is substantially restricted.

Liam also experiences symptoms of low mood and reduced motivation consistent with depression, including social withdrawal, limited engagement in meaningful activities and reduced drive to initiate tasks independently. These symptoms further impact Liam’s capacity to maintain routines, engage socially and participate consistently in daily life without prompting and support.

Overall, Liam’s diagnoses result in pervasive and interrelated impairments across cognitive, emotional, motivational and psychosocial domains. While Liam retains strengths in communication and insight, these strengths do not offset the cumulative functional impact of his conditions. His presentation reflects a chronic psychosocial disability that significantly limits his independence and necessitates ongoing structured supports to maintain safety, stability and quality of life.

## WHODAS 2.0 {#whodas-2.0}

The adult World Health Organisation Disability Assessment Schedule 2.0 (WHODAS 2.0) is a 36-item standardised measure assessing disability across six functional domains: understanding and communicating, getting around, self-care, getting along with people, life activities and participation in society. It is recognised by the NDIS as a best-practice tool for evaluating functional impairment related to psychosocial, cognitive and neurodevelopmental disability. Raw and domain scores reflect the severity of functional impact, with higher scores indicating greater levels of disability.

Liam’s WHODAS 2.0 results indicate an overall severe level of functional impairment, with particularly significant restrictions evident in life activities, cognition, mobility and participation in society. His scores are as follows:

* **Understanding and Communicating: 70/100 (70%)** This score reflects severe impairment in Liam’s cognitive functioning, including concentration, memory, learning, problem-solving and communication. Liam experiences significant difficulty sustaining attention, remembering to complete important tasks, analysing problems and learning or navigating new situations. These impairments are consistent with the combined effects of schizoaffective disorder, long-term medication use, chronic fatigue and sleep disturbance. Functionally, Liam relies heavily on external prompting and structure to manage daily routines, appointments and household responsibilities and is unable to consistently initiate or follow through with tasks independently.  
* **Getting Around: 75/100 (75%)** Liam’s score indicates severe impairment in mobility-related functioning. While he is physically able to ambulate and transfer independently, his functional mobility is significantly limited by fatigue, reduced motivation, anxiety and reliance on others for transport. Liam experiences difficulty leaving the house, walking longer distances and sustaining community mobility without assistance. As a result, he is dependent on his carer for most transport and community access, with limited capacity to independently initiate outings.  
* **Self-Care: 50/100 (50%)** This score reflects moderate impairment in self-care activities. Liam demonstrates difficulty maintaining consistent personal hygiene, dressing and eating routines without prompting. While he retains the physical skills required for self-care, fatigue, cognitive slowing and reduced initiation significantly impact his consistency. Without structured reminders and support, personal care tasks are frequently delayed, forgotten or deprioritised.  
* **Getting Along with People: 75/100 (75%)** Liam’s score indicates moderate impairment in interpersonal functioning. He experiences difficulty engaging with unfamiliar people, maintaining friendships and initiating social interactions. Anxiety, sensory sensitivities and reduced social tolerance contribute to avoidance of social situations, particularly in busy or unpredictable environments. As a result, Liam has limited social connections and relies primarily on paid supports for interaction, increasing his risk of social isolation.  
* **Life Activities: 90/100 (90%)** This score represents Liam’s most significant area of impairment and reflects severe difficulty managing domestic and daily life activities. Liam is unable to independently manage meal preparation, cleaning, laundry, household organisation or financial tasks due to reduced energy, poor task initiation, cognitive impairment and fatigue. Tasks requiring planning, sequencing and sustained effort are particularly compromised, resulting in a high level of dependence on his carer and NDIS-funded services to maintain daily functioning.  
* **Participation in Society: 75/100 (75%)** Liam’s score reflects severe restriction in his ability to participate in community life. He experiences significant barriers to engaging in social, recreational and community activities due to fatigue, anxiety, sensory sensitivities, transport dependence and reduced motivation. Without structured support, Liam becomes socially withdrawn and disengaged from community participation, further limiting opportunities for meaningful engagement and quality of life.

Overall, Liam’s WHODAS 2.0 profile demonstrates a high level of disability, with severe functional impairment across multiple domains and an overall disability score of 73/100 (98.1st percentile). His greatest areas of disability are evident in life activities, cognition, mobility and participation in society, reflecting the pervasive impact of his psychosocial disability on daily living and community functioning.

These findings are consistent with Liam’s observed functional presentation and strongly support the ongoing need for structured, reliable supports, including support worker assistance, transport supports and coordinated services. Without sustained intervention, Liam is at risk of further functional decline, increased isolation and reduced capacity to maintain safe and consistent daily routines.

## Adolescent/Adult Sensory Profile {#adolescent/adult-sensory-profile}

The Adolescent/Adult Sensory Profile (ASP) is a standardised self-report questionnaire used to evaluate an individual’s sensory processing patterns in everyday environments. It measures behavioural responses to sensory input across key sensory domains, including auditory, visual, tactile, movement and activity level, organised into four sensory processing quadrants: Low Registration, Sensation Seeking, Sensory Sensitivity and Sensation Avoiding. Scores are compared with same-aged peers, with results indicating whether an individual experiences sensory input similarly to others or demonstrates increased sensory seeking, sensitivity, avoidance or reduced sensory awareness. The ASP assists clinicians in identifying sensory modulation differences that may impact attention, emotional regulation, task engagement and participation in daily activities.

Liam’s results on the Adolescent/Adult Sensory Profile revealed the following:

**Low Registration:** Much more than most people  
Liam demonstrates significantly reduced awareness of sensory input, indicating a high neurological threshold for sensory stimuli. He frequently misses or is slow to respond to environmental cues, internal body signals and task-related prompts that others typically notice. Functionally, this contributes to difficulty initiating tasks, reduced responsiveness to verbal cues, forgetting to commence activities and appearing disengaged or unmotivated. Liam requires increased prompting, repetition and external structure to support task initiation and follow-through, particularly for daily routines and self-care activities.

**Sensation Avoiding:** Much more than most people  
Liam experiences environments as overwhelming and actively attempts to reduce sensory input in order to remain regulated. He demonstrates a strong preference for quiet, predictable, low-stimulation environments and actively avoids busy, noisy or crowded settings. This sensory avoidance is evident in his avoidance of shopping centres during peak times, withdrawal from community environments and preference to remain at home. Sensory overload contributes to increased stress, fatigue and withdrawal, significantly limiting Liam’s capacity to participate in community activities and sustain engagement outside familiar environments.

**Sensory Sensitivity:** Much more than most people  
Liam demonstrates heightened reactivity to sensory input, particularly auditory, visual and movement-related stimuli. He is easily distracted by background noise, startled by unexpected sounds, overwhelmed by fast-moving or visually busy environments and distressed by excessive sensory input. These sensitivities interfere with concentration, increase cognitive fatigue and reduce Liam’s tolerance for busy or unpredictable environments. As a result, Liam experiences difficulty sustaining attention, remaining regulated and completing tasks in environments with competing sensory demands.

**Sensation Seeking:** Much less than most people  
Liam demonstrates significantly reduced sensory-seeking behaviours. He does not actively seek sensory input to increase alertness, engagement or arousal and instead prefers low-demand, passive activities. This contributes to low spontaneous activity levels, limited exploration and reduced motivation to initiate tasks independently. Liam is unlikely to use sensory input as a self-regulation strategy to increase energy or focus, further impacting task initiation, activity engagement and follow-through.

Liam’s sensory profile demonstrates a complex pattern of Low Registration, high Sensory Sensitivity and marked Sensation Avoiding, combined with low Sensation Seeking. Functionally, this means Liam both misses important sensory cues needed to initiate tasks and becomes easily overwhelmed when sensory demands increase. This combination significantly impacts his ability to sustain attention, regulate emotions, tolerate busy environments and participate consistently in daily and community activities.

Liam may appear disengaged or unresponsive when sensory input is insufficient, yet become stressed, fatigued and withdrawn when environmental stimulation increases. His ability to remain regulated and function effectively is highly dependent on predictable routines, structured environments, reduced sensory load, careful pacing and consistent external prompting.

These sensory processing differences directly contribute to Liam’s difficulties with self-care consistency, community access, emotional regulation, fatigue management and reliance on formal supports. The findings strongly support the need for ongoing structured supports and environmental accommodations to maintain Liam’s functional stability and participation in daily life.

Without structured, reliable support, Liam would be unable to maintain personal care routines, nutrition, household management, medication adherence or community engagement at a safe and sustainable level. The absence of informal supports further increases his vulnerability and reliance on NDIS-funded services.

Overall, Liam’s functional impairments are chronic, interrelated and unlikely to significantly improve without ongoing support. His presentation does not support a reduction in supports but rather, it highlights the need for consistent, well-coordinated assistance to maintain stability, prevent deterioration and support quality of life.

## Recommendations {#recommendations}

Based on the clinical assessment and standardised tools, the following recommendations are made to support independence, safety and wellbeing:

**1\. Support Worker Assistance**

Ongoing support worker assistance is recommended to support Liam with activities of daily living and community participation. Liam’s severe impairments in life activities, cognition, mobility and participation, as evidenced by the WHODAS 2.0, indicate a continued need for hands-on assistance and structured prompting.

Support worker assistance should focus on:

* Prompting and support with personal care routines (showering, dressing, grooming)  
* Meal preparation and prompting to eat regularly  
* Laundry and household task completion  
* Support with shopping and community access, particularly during low-stimulation times  
* Transport assistance and accompaniment to appointments and essential outings  
* Emotional support, reassurance and pacing during periods of fatigue or overwhelm

Consistent support worker involvement is essential to prevent self-neglect, reduce stress and maintain Liam’s functional baseline.

**2\. Support Coordination**

Ongoing support coordination is strongly recommended due to Liam’s limited capacity to independently manage services, navigate systems and advocate for himself. Cognitive fatigue, reduced executive functioning and low motivation significantly impair his ability to coordinate supports without assistance.

Support coordination should focus on:

* Monitoring plan utilisation and preventing under- or over-spend  
* Coordinating support worker services and rostering  
* Supporting access to transport and community supports  
* Assisting with problem-solving when services change or issues arise  
* Ensuring supports remain aligned with Liam’s fluctuating needs

**3\. Transport Funding**

An increase in transport funding is recommended to support Liam’s goal of increased independence and community access. Liam does not drive and relies primarily on his carer for transport. While he is occasionally able to use Uber services, current transport funding limits his ability to access the community independently when carer support is unavailable.

Increased transport funding would:

* Reduce reliance on carers for all transport needs  
* Support access to appointments, shopping and community activities  
* Increase flexibility and autonomy within Liam’s daily life

**4\. Cleaning Services**

Continuation of NDIS-funded cleaning services is recommended to maintain a safe and hygienic home environment. Liam’s severe impairment in life activities, combined with fatigue and reduced task initiation, means he is unable to independently maintain household cleanliness without support.

**5\. Gardening and Home Maintenance**

Continuation of NDIS-funded gardening and assistance with home maintenance is recommended. Liam lacks the energy, motivation and executive capacity to manage outdoor maintenance tasks independently and without these supports his home environment would quickly become unmanageable.

**6\. Occupational Therapy** 

Ongoing occupational therapy is recommended to support Liam to maintain functional stability, optimise daily routines and reduce the impact of fatigue, sensory sensitivities and cognitive load on everyday functioning. Liam’s WHODAS 2.0 results indicate severe impairment in life activities, cognition, mobility and participation and his Adolescent/Adult Sensory Profile demonstrates significant sensory processing differences that directly impact task initiation, emotional regulation and community access.

Occupational therapy would focus on maintenance, environmental adaptation and functional compensation, rather than expectation of full independence. Recommended areas of focus include:

* Development and refinement of structured daily routines to support personal care, meals, sleep hygiene and household tasks  
* Executive functioning strategies, including task breakdown, visual supports, reminders and simplified systems to reduce cognitive load  
* Sensory regulation strategies to support emotional regulation, fatigue management and tolerance of community environments  
* Environmental modification and pacing strategies to reduce sensory overload and exhaustion  
* Support for community participation planning, including timing, preparation and recovery strategies

Ongoing occupational therapy input would support Liam to sustain his current level of functioning, reduce reliance on crisis-driven supports and prevent functional decline associated with fatigue, sensory overload and reduced initiation capacity.

**7\. Psychology** 

Ongoing psychological support is recommended to support Liam’s mental health stability, emotional wellbeing and coping capacity in the context of chronic psychosocial disability. Liam experiences longstanding anxiety, depression, social withdrawal and stress related to fatigue, sleep disturbance and environmental overwhelm. These factors significantly impact his motivation, emotional regulation and engagement in daily life.

Psychology support should be supportive and stabilisation-focused, rather than intensive trauma or skills-based intervention and may include:

* Support to manage anxiety, low mood and stress associated with daily functioning  
* Development of coping strategies to manage fatigue, overwhelm and sensory stressors  
* Psychoeducation regarding the interaction between sleep, medication effects, mental health and functioning  
* Support to reduce social withdrawal and improve emotional resilience  
* Coordination with other supports (e.g. OT, support workers) to ensure consistent, realistic expectations

Given Liam’s limited informal supports and reliance on paid services, psychology input would provide an important layer of emotional support and monitoring, reducing risk of deterioration and supporting long-term stability.
