# **Report Overview** {#report-overview}

This report has been prepared based on the information available at the time of assessment, consultation, intervention, and clinical analysis of the client’s goals, circumstances, and needs. While the information is accurate at the time of writing, it should be recognised that many factors can influence a person’s function and performance, and further assessment may be required in the future.

Occupational Therapy Melbourne acknowledges that some aspects of this report may highlight areas of functional difficulty, which may feel confronting to clients and their support. The intention of this report is not to focus on deficits, but rather to identify opportunities for support and recommend strategies and resources that enable the client to live as independently, safely, and meaningfully as possible.

This report is prepared for the named client and relevant stakeholders for the purpose of supporting service delivery, funding, and planning. It contains confidential and privileged information and should be used only for its intended purpose.

# **Part A: About The Participant** {#part-a:-about-the-participant}

## Primary Diagnosis and Medical History  {#primary-diagnosis-and-medical-history }

Daniel presents with a complex neurodevelopmental profile in which the most functionally significant conditions are Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD), Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD), Anxiety, and Scoliosis.

His diagnosis of ASD is reflected in marked differences in social communication, pragmatic language, and sensory processing. Daniel experiences heightened sensitivity to light and noise, relies heavily on predictable routines, and finds it challenging to initiate or sustain reciprocal interactions. These features have a profound impact on his ability to participate in community settings and to manage everyday activities that involve unfamiliar people or environments. 

ADHD further contributes to persistent executive functioning difficulties, including planning, organisation, and task initiation. This combination of autism and ADHD results in low motivation for self-care and domestic tasks, difficulty sustaining attention on non-preferred activities, and a need for frequent prompting to complete daily routines.

Daniel’s co-occurring anxiety amplifies these challenges, particularly in social or unpredictable situations. He reports feeling nervous when interacting with others and frequently avoids community environments where sensory demands and uncertainty are high. His anxiety not only limits his participation outside the home but also compounds the executive functioning barriers associated with ADHD, making transitions and multi-step tasks more overwhelming.

Alongside these neurodevelopmental conditions, Daniel presents with scoliosis characterised by a persistent left-sided spinal curvature and reduced muscle tone. This contributes to a postural twist, impaired coordination, and increased fatigue, reducing his endurance for community mobility and placing him at risk of musculoskeletal discomfort.

It was also noted that Daniel has experienced feeding difficulties since birth, and this continues to be an ongoing issue. He demonstrates significant dietary restrictions and avoidance of many foods secondary to sensory sensitivities and strong food preferences. These limitations contribute to a restricted diet, reduced nutritional variety, and increased dependence on his mother for meal preparation. 

Additional medical history includes language delays, verbal dyspraxia, phonological disorder, pragmatic language disorder, and very low auditory processing ability. While these diagnoses provide important context to Daniel’s developmental trajectory, their current functional impact is secondary to the primary conditions outlined above.

## Social Background {#social-background}

Daniel is a young adult residing with his mother David in a two-bedroom apartment in Bentleigh, Victoria. His younger brother lives in the home approximately 80% of the time and provides some support. Daniel has lived in the Bentleigh area for most of his life and describes it as a familiar and comfortable environment.

Daniel is currently not engaged in employment or formal education. He previously worked for two years but ceased employment due to difficulties with workplace interactions and the associated anxiety. He now receives a pension and has no immediate plans to return to work.

Social engagement is noted to be limited. Daniel maintains some online friendships but has minimal in-person social contact outside of family. He reports feeling nervous when interacting with people and typically leaves the home only once a week, usually for appointments or essential errands. Daniel and David report extended family relationships are mixed, there is some contact with grandparents on his father’s side, but there is no contact with his father due to a family violence order in place. 

## Formal Supports {#formal-supports}

Daniel is currently only linked with a General Practitioner for routine medical care. He is not currently engaged with any allied-health or specialist services which would benefit his care. 

# **Part B: Mental Health And Psychosocial Functioning** {#part-b:-mental-health-and-psychosocial-functioning}

## Emotional Regulation {#emotional-regulation}

Daniel’s emotional regulation is significantly influenced by the combined effects of Autism Spectrum Disorder, ADHD, and anxiety. He experiences high baseline anxiety, particularly in situations involving social interaction, unpredictable routines, or strong sensory input such as bright light and background noise. When confronted with these triggers, Daniel frequently becomes physically tense and mentally overwhelmed, leading to avoidance of community environments and increased reliance on his mother for reassurance.

Although he rarely exhibits outward aggression, he demonstrates internalising behaviours such as withdrawal, quiet distress, and loss of motivation when overwhelmed. Executive functioning deficits associated with ADHD further compound these challenges by making it difficult for Daniel to plan coping strategies, shift attention away from stressors, or recover quickly after periods of dysregulation.

Despite these vulnerabilities, Daniel displays insight and self-awareness by recognising the situations that heighten his anxiety and occasionally verbalising a need for space or comfort. This emerging self-advocacy is a key strength that can be built upon in therapy.

## Psychosocial Functioning {#psychosocial-functioning}

Daniel’s psychosocial functioning is characterised by a strong preference for familiar environments and solitary pursuits. He enjoys structured, interest-based activities such as geology, science, video games, and digital art, which provide both intellectual stimulation and a sense of control. However, his opportunities for social connection are limited. He maintains a small network of online friends but rarely engages in face-to-face friendships or community groups.

Daniel’s anxiety and sensory sensitivities result in him leaving the house around once per week, typically for medical appointments or essential shopping with his mother. Even in these situations he prefers quiet, low-stimulus settings such as small cafés with predictable routines. He demonstrates interest in expanding his community participation but is hesitant to initiate new activities without structured support.

Mitchells’ family dynamics were noted to be generally positive and protective. His mother provides daily practical and emotional support, while his younger brother offers incidental companionship. There is no contact with his father due to a family violence order. Daniel’s sense of safety and trust is therefore closely tied to his immediate household.

## Behavioural Presentation {#behavioural-presentation}

During assessment, Daniel presented as polite, cooperative, and softly spoken. He engaged willingly in conversation as the environment was calm and familiar, displaying articulate language when discussing topics of interest.

In high-stimulus settings Daniel is likely to become quiet, withdrawn, or avoidant rather than oppositional. These behaviours are best understood as protective responses to anxiety and sensory overload rather than deliberate non-compliance. With predictable routines, clear instructions, and gradual exposure, Daniel is capable of maintaining regulation and demonstrating his cognitive strengths.

# **Part D: Assessment Findings** {#part-d:-assessment-findings}

Two standardised assessments were administered as part of this Functional Capacity Assessment:

## WHODAS2.0 {#whodas2.0}

The adult version of the WHODAS 2.0 is a 36-item measure that assesses disability in adults aged 18 years and older. It evaluates disability across six domains which include understanding and communicating, getting around, self-care, getting along with people, life activities and participation in society. The WHODAS 2.0 is recognised by the NDIS as a best-practice assessment for individuals with a primary disability.

The outcome of the WHODAS 2.0 provides a percentage of impairment based on the results of the questions answered, with 100% indicating the highest level of identified disability.

* **Understanding and Communicating:** Daniel scored 18/24 (75%) disability in this subdomain, which assesses the ability to understand information and communicate effectively. This reflects marked difficulty processing multi-step instructions, engaging in abstract reasoning, and maintaining conversation in noisy or unpredictable settings.  
* **Getting Around:** Daniel scored 13/20 (65%) disability in this subdomain, which examines a person’s ability to move about their environment. He is independently mobile but experiences reduced endurance, a persistent left-sided twist, and low muscle tone associated with scoliosis, resulting in fatigue and avoidance of prolonged outings.  
* **Self-Care:** Daniel scored 13/16 (81%) disability in this subdomain, which evaluates a person’s ability to engage in their own hygiene and daily self-care. Although physically capable, he requires frequent prompting to shower, dress, and brush his teeth, with low motivation and sensory sensitivities impacting regularity.  
* **Getting Along with People:** Daniel scored 14/20 (70%) disability in this subdomain, which looks at an individual’s ability to interact with others. He experiences significant anxiety and pragmatic language challenges that limit social engagement, relying on online interactions and avoiding group settings.  
* **Life Activities:** Daniel scored 16/16 (100%) disability in this subdomain, which measures capacity for domestic and work-related tasks. He is completely dependent on his mother for household routines such as cooking, cleaning, and laundry, with executive functioning deficits and sensory aversions preventing independent task completion.  
* **Participation in Society:** Daniel scored 21/32 (66%) disability in this subdomain, which assesses an individual’s ability to participate in community and social activities. He rarely leaves home, requires accompaniment for most outings, and avoids public transport due to anxiety and sensory overload.

The above assessment was completed by Daniel, who scored 95 out of 128 (74%) overall on the WHODAS 2.0, which places him in the moderate to severe range of functional disability. These findings highlight Daniel’s greatest barriers in life activities, self-care, and understanding and communicating, with significant limitations also evident in community participation and social interaction.

## Vinelands-3 Domain Level Parent/Caregiver  {#vinelands-3-domain-level-parent/caregiver}

The Vineland-3 Domain-Level Parent/Caregiver Form was completed with Daniel’s mother to evaluate his adaptive functioning. The Vineland-3 provides norm-referenced scores (Mean \= 100, SD \= 15), with lower scores reflecting reduced functional independence across everyday skills.  
Daniel’s results were as follows:

* **Communication:** Standard Score \= 40 (percentile \<1)  
  Falls in the low range, indicating severe difficulty understanding and using language in daily contexts.  
  Daniel can follow simple, familiar instructions but struggles with multi-step or abstract directions and has marked difficulty sustaining conversation, summarising information, and providing context.  
  Very low auditory processing and social anxiety further limit his ability to comprehend and express information, particularly in busy or unpredictable environments.  
* **Daily Living Skills:** Standard Score \= 31 (percentile \<1)  
  Falls in the low range, reflecting profound limitations in age-appropriate independence.  
  Daniel requires frequent prompting for all self-care routines, including showering, dressing, and grooming, and is dependent on his mother for meal preparation, household chores, and community-based tasks.  
  Executive functioning deficits and germ aversion significantly restrict his ability to initiate, plan, and sequence everyday activities.  
* **Socialisation:** Standard Score \= 34 (percentile \<1)  
  Falls in the low range, indicating severe difficulty in forming and maintaining social relationships.  
  Daniel rarely engages in in-person friendships and relies primarily on online interactions.  
  He shows limited ability to interpret social cues, regulate conversation, or participate in group activities, with anxiety and sensory sensitivities contributing to social withdrawal.

**Maladaptive Behaviour Scales**  
As part of the Vineland-3, Daniel’s mother also completed the Maladaptive Behaviour Scales, which assess emotional and behavioural concerns that interfere with adaptive functioning.

* **Internalising** (v-scale score \= 23\)  
  This clinically significant score indicates elevated internalising behaviours such as anxiety, emotional withdrawal, and worry. These findings are consistent with Daniel’s history of high baseline anxiety, sensory sensitivities, and avoidance of community settings.  
* **Externalising** (v-scale score \= 18\)  
  This score falls in the elevated range, suggesting occasional behavioural rigidity or oppositional behaviours, though Daniel is generally described as polite and cooperative in familiar, structured environments.

Daniel’s Vineland-3 results indicate global adaptive functioning delays, with the most pronounced challenges in Daily Living Skills and Socialisation, both within the low range. Communication, while a relative strength within his own profile, remains severely below age expectations. 

The presence of clinically significant internalising behaviours underscores the impact of anxiety and sensory sensitivities on Daniel’s daily functioning and supports the need for comprehensive multidisciplinary intervention across occupational therapy, psychology, physiotherapy, and support work to promote independence and community engagement.

## Summary of Functional Impairments {#summary-of-functional-impairments}

Daniel is a young adult with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD), Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD), anxiety, and scoliosis, who presents with global adaptive delays and significant executive functioning and sensory processing difficulties.

His WHODAS 2.0 score of 95/128 (74% disability) indicates moderate-to-severe impairment across all domains, with greatest barriers in Life Activities (100% disability), Self-Care (81% disability), and Understanding & Communicating (75% disability). Similarly, his Vineland-3 Adaptive Behaviour Composite of 40 (\<1st percentile) confirms functioning well below age expectations, with profound limitations in Daily Living Skills (SS \= 31\) and Socialisation (SS \= 34), and severe impairment in Communication (SS \= 40).

Daniel requires frequent prompting and hands-on support to complete basic self-care tasks such as showering, dressing, and grooming, and is fully dependent on his mother for domestic routines including cooking, cleaning, and laundry. Executive dysfunction and germ aversion further contribute to avoidance of household responsibilities. Communication is characterised by basic expressive ability but poor pragmatic skills, with difficulty following multi-step instructions, summarising information, and clearly expressing needs in busy or noisy environments.

Mobility is physically intact for short distances, but scoliosis, reduced muscle tone, and a left-sided postural twist result in fatigue and avoidance of community outings. Daniel leaves the home only once per week and has not independently used public transport in several years. Social participation is extremely limited, with reliance on online friendships and withdrawal from face-to-face interactions due to anxiety and sensory overload.

Emotional wellbeing is further impacted by clinically significant internalising behaviours (Vineland v-scale \= 23), reflecting high anxiety, emotional withdrawal, and low confidence in new situations. While Daniel presents as polite and cooperative in familiar environments, he is easily overwhelmed in unstructured or overstimulating contexts.

Overall, Daniel’s functional impairments span self-care, domestic life, communication, mobility, social participation, and emotional regulation, resulting in a high level of dependence on his mother for daily routines and community access. These findings highlight the need for coordinated, multidisciplinary supports including occupational therapy, physiotherapy, psychology, speech pathology and structured support worker hours to build capacity, promote independence, and increase engagement in meaningful home and community activities. 

## Recommendations {#recommendations}

Based on the clinical assessment and standardised tools, the following recommendations are made to support independence, safety, and wellbeing:

**1\. Support Worker**

Daniel does not currently access any formal support worker hours. Given his significant dependence on his mother for personal care prompting, domestic tasks, and all community access, this level of informal support is insufficient to meet his functional needs. He requires regular support to safely build independence and participate in meaningful activities.

Recommended Use of Hours:

* Assistance with meal preparation and light domestic tasks using step-by-step guidance to develop confidence and sequencing skills.  
* Community access (e.g., visiting cafés, art galleries, science museums, or trivia nights) with gradual exposure to social settings to build tolerance for sensory input and reduce anxiety.  
* Support with transport training, including graded reintroduction to trains or buses to rebuild public transport confidence.  
* Development of weekly routines for shopping, bill payment, and simple errands to decrease reliance on his mother.

Regular support worker assistance will enable Daniel to develop domestic skills, improve social participation, and reduce long-term caregiver burden on his mother.

**2\. Occupational Therapy**

Daniel requires ongoing OT to address barriers related to executive functioning, sensory sensitivities, and reduced independence in self-care and domestic activities.

Key OT Interventions:

* Development of structured routines for showering, dressing, and grooming, including visual schedules and environmental cues.  
* Executive function training to support task initiation, sequencing, and planning for household tasks and appointments.  
* Sensory modulation strategies to manage light sensitivity, germ aversion, and tactile defensiveness.  
* Trials of assistive equipment such as modified cutlery or ergonomic tools to accommodate fine motor weakness and reduce fatigue.

Regular OT input will improve Daniel’s capacity to manage daily activities, decrease anxiety around hygiene and domestic tasks, and build the skills needed for greater independence.

**3\. Physiotherapy**

Daniel’s scoliosis, reduced muscle tone, and left-sided postural twist place him at risk of ongoing musculoskeletal discomfort and reduced endurance.

Recommended Supports:

* Comprehensive gait and posture assessment.  
* Individualised strengthening and stretching program to improve core stability and functional endurance.  
* Education in fatigue management and safe movement strategies for daily tasks and community outings.

Physiotherapy will help reduce pain, improve posture, and prevent further functional decline, enabling Daniel to engage more confidently in community activities.

**4\. Psychology**

Daniel experiences significant anxiety that restricts his community participation and daily functioning.

Recommended Supports:

* Ongoing psychological therapy to address anxiety using cognitive behavioural strategies and graded exposure techniques.  
* Emotional regulation support to build coping skills for sensory overload and unexpected changes in routine.  
* Goal-setting sessions to enhance motivation for independent living and engagement in meaningful activities.

Psychological support will improve Daniel’s confidence, reduce avoidance behaviours, and facilitate increased participation in social and domestic tasks.

**5\. Speech Pathology**

Given Daniel’s history of verbal dyspraxia, phonological disorder, and ongoing pragmatic communication difficulties, speech pathology input is recommended.

Key Areas of Focus:

* Assessment of swallowing safety due to early feeding difficulties and ongoing coordination concerns.  
* Therapy to improve pragmatic communication and conversational reciprocity to support social engagement.

Speech pathology will enhance Daniel’s functional communication and reduce risks associated with eating and drinking.

**6\. Dietitian**

Daniel’s restricted diet and limited participation in meal preparation place him at risk of poor nutrition.

Recommended Supports:

* Review of dietary intake and nutritional adequacy.  
* Structured meal planning strategies that account for sensory sensitivities and low motivation.  
* Exploration of pre-prepared meal options to ensure safe and consistent access to balanced nutrition.

Dietetic support will help maintain Daniel’s health, improve food variety, and reduce reliance on his mother for meal preparation.

**7\. Environmental and Consumable Supports**

Daniel’s sensory sensitivities, particularly light sensitivity, warrant environmental modifications.

Recommended Items:

* Window tinting or light-filtering solutions to improve comfort and reduce sensory overload in the home.  
* Outdoor blinds that will provide a shaded area on the balcony to encourage increased time outside and reduce sensory overload.     
* A blade free fan in Daniel’s bedroom as he struggles with the heat.   
* Sensory regulation items such as weighted blankets or noise-cancelling headphones to support self-soothing and anxiety management.  
* Pre-prepared meal packages to supplement dietary intake and reduce the need for complex meal preparation.

These consumables will reduce environmental triggers, improve daily comfort, and enhance participation in home-based activities.

**8\. Support Coordination** 

Daniel would benefit from access to a support coordinator to ensure effective implementation of his NDIS plan and to navigate available services. 

Recommended Areas of Focus: 

* Implementation of funded supports and assistance with linking to allied health providers   
* Assistance to identify and connect with appropriate social and recreational opportunities.  
* Guidance with plan navigation and monitoring to ensure Daniel’s supports remain adequate and responsive to his needs. 

Support coordination will ensure Daniel’s plan is effectively implemented and that his transition to greater independence is supported in a structured and sustainable way.
