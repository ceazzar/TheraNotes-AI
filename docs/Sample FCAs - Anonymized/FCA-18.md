## Primary Diagnosis and Medical History  {#primary-diagnosis-and-medical-history }

Sophie has a documented history of Mild Intellectual Disability and speech delay, both of which have an ongoing impact on his functional capacity across daily living, communication, decision-making and community participation. He also experiences difficulty comprehending verbal information, particularly when information is delivered quickly, uses unfamiliar language or involves administrative or abstract concepts. The interaction between Sophie’s cognitive impairment, communication difficulties and physical discomfort results in a reduced overall functional baseline. While he is capable of completing some tasks independently in familiar and predictable contexts, his capacity deteriorates significantly when tasks require planning, judgement, communication or sustained physical effort.

## WHODAS 2.0 {#whodas-2.0}

The WHODAS 2.0 is a standardised assessment measuring disability across six functional domains: understanding and communicating, getting around, self-care, getting along with people, life activities, and participation in society. Higher scores indicate greater functional impairment.

Sophie’s WHODAS 2.0 results indicate a high level of functional impairment across multiple domains, particularly in interpersonal functioning, community participation, life activities and communication, consistent with the functional presentation observed during assessment.

* **Understanding and Communicating:** Sophie scored 19/24 (79%), indicating severe difficulty in this domain. This reflects significant challenges with understanding verbal information, processing instructions, communicating needs and engaging in complex conversations. These difficulties are consistent with Sophie’s longstanding speech delay and Mild Intellectual Disability and directly impact his ability to advocate for himself, participate in service-related communication and manage daily problem-solving independently.  
* **Getting Around:** Sophie scored 17/20 (85%), reflecting severe functional limitation in mobility-related activities. While he is physically able to mobilise, this score reflects his inability to navigate the community independently due to cognitive impairment, reduced orientation skills and safety awareness. Sophie requires support worker assistance for all community mobility and transport to prevent disorientation and risk.  
* **Self-Care:** Sophie scored 7/16 (44%), indicating mild to moderate impairment in self-care. This aligns with observed relative independence in basic personal care tasks such as showering, dressing and toileting. However, this score reflects variability in performance and reliance on routine, prompting and support to maintain consistency, particularly when pain, fatigue or disrupted sleep routines are present.  
* **Getting Along with People:** Sophie scored 20/20 (100%), indicating extreme difficulty in this domain. This reflects significant challenges with social interaction, communication reciprocity, managing frustration and engaging effectively with others, particularly unfamiliar people. His communication impairment and reduced social understanding significantly limit his capacity to form relationships, resolve interpersonal difficulties and participate in social contexts without structured support.  
* **Life Activities:** Sophie scored 15/16 (94%), reflecting severe impairment in managing domestic responsibilities and daily life activities. This score is consistent with his dependence on support workers for meal preparation, cleaning, shopping, transport and organisation of daily routines. Cognitive limitations and chronic back pain significantly restrict his ability to independently manage household tasks.  
* **Participation in Society:** Sophie scored 31/32 (97%), indicating extreme restriction in community participation. This reflects his inability to independently access community environments, participate in social, recreational or civic activities and maintain meaningful engagement without substantial support. Social isolation, lack of informal supports and absence of weekend support further compound these restrictions.

Sophie’s WHODAS profile reflects a severe to extreme level of disability across most domains, with relative strength only in basic self-care tasks with a total score of 109/128 (85%) disability. His results highlight limitations in communication, social interaction, community participation and life activities, strongly supporting the need for ongoing Core Supports, capacity-building interventions and advocacy-based assistance to maintain safety, independence and quality of life.

## CHIEF-SF {#chief-sf}

The CHIEF-SF was completed by Sophie as part of this assessment to evaluate the frequency and severity of environmental barriers impacting his ability to engage in everyday activities. This tool explores five domains: Policies, Physical Environment, Attitudes and Support, Services and Assistance and Information/Communication. Each item is rated based on how often the barrier occurs and the degree to which it interferes with participation.

Sophie’s results indicate moderate to high levels of environmental interference, with the most significant barriers identified in the areas of transportation and physical environment, information and communication accessibility, attitudes of others, and service systems.

* **Transportation and physical environment** emerged as a major environmental barrier for Sophie. He reported frequent difficulty accessing transportation, which significantly limits his ability to attend appointments, access the community and participate in meaningful activities. Sophie is unable to use public transport independently due to cognitive and communication impairments, and the lack of accessible, supported transport substantially restricts his participation in daily life. These barriers occur regularly and represent a significant problem, reinforcing his reliance on support workers for all community mobility.  
* **Information and communication accessibility** was identified as a frequent and significant barrier. Sophie reported difficulty accessing information in formats he can understand or use, particularly in relation to services, healthcare and administrative systems. This reflects the functional impact of his speech delay and Mild Intellectual Disability, which limit his ability to comprehend complex or abstract information. Inaccessible communication contributes to service breakdowns, missed opportunities for support and increased dependence on advocacy.  
* **Attitudes and support from others** were also identified as a source of frequent difficulty. Sophie experiences challenges when interacting with unfamiliar people who struggle to understand his speech or communication style. These interactions often lead to frustration, withdrawal and reduced confidence engaging in community or service environments. Attitudinal barriers further limit Sophie’s ability to advocate for himself and participate independently.  
* **Services and assistance** were rated as a significant barrier. Sophie reported difficulty obtaining help when needed, particularly outside limited weekday support hours. Given his complete absence of informal supports, gaps or inconsistencies in formal services have a disproportionate impact on his functioning. Limited support availability, particularly on weekends, directly restricts Sophie’s ability to participate in sporting clubs, community activities and social opportunities.  
* **Policies and systems** were identified as a moderate barrier. Sophie reported that rules and requirements of organisations and government programs frequently make it difficult for him to do what he wants or needs to do. These systemic barriers align with Sophie’s previous experiences of service coordination failures and administrative difficulties, which he was unable to identify or resolve independently due to cognitive and communication impairments.

Overall, Sophie’s CHIEF-SF results demonstrate that environmental barriers play a substantial role in limiting his participation, compounding the functional impacts of his disability. Frequent barriers related to transport, inaccessible information, attitudes of others and service systems significantly restrict Sophie’s independence and community engagement. These findings strongly support the need for ongoing support worker assistance, active support coordination and capacity-building supports to reduce environmental barriers and enable safe, meaningful participation in daily life.

## Recommendations {#recommendations}

Based on the clinical assessment and standardised tools, the following recommendations are made to support  independence, safety, and wellbeing:

**1\. Support Worker Assistance**

Sophie requires increased and ongoing support worker assistance to maintain functional stability across domestic living, community access, decision-making and capacity building. His Mild Intellectual Disability, communication impairment and chronic back pain significantly limit his ability to independently plan, initiate and complete daily activities, particularly those that require judgement, sequencing or sustained effort.

Current support levels of approximately 6–7 hours per week on weekdays are insufficient to meet Sophie’s assessed needs. Without adequate support, Sophie is unable to maintain consistent routines, access the community safely or engage in meaningful activities, placing him at risk of isolation and unmet daily living needs.

Recommended focus areas include:

* Assistance with meal preparation, grocery shopping and establishment of consistent, healthy eating routines  
* Support with cleaning, laundry initiation and home management tasks  
* Community access support for appointments, shopping and recreational activities  
* Capacity building to support independent living skills, routine development and confidence  
* Weekend support to enable participation in cricket, football clubs and other community-based activities aligned with Sophie’s interests  
* Transport assistance and supervision to ensure safe navigation in the community

Increased support worker hours, including weekend availability, are essential to promote independence, prevent isolation and support Sophie’s participation in meaningful occupations.

**2\. Speech Pathology**

Speech pathology intervention is strongly recommended to address Sophie’s significant expressive and receptive communication impairments resulting from longstanding speech delay. His communication difficulties substantially limit his ability to advocate for himself, understand information, participate in service-related conversations and engage confidently in the community.

Recommended areas of focus include:

* Functional communication strategies to improve clarity of speech and intelligibility  
* Support for understanding and responding to verbal information  
* Development of alternative or supported communication strategies where required  
* Strategies to reduce frustration during communication breakdowns  
* Capacity building to support Sophie’s ability to express preferences, consent and needs

Speech pathology input is essential to improve Sophie’s functional communication, reduce vulnerability and enable more meaningful participation in daily life and service systems.

**3\. Occupational Therapy**

Ongoing occupational therapy is recommended to continue supporting Sophie’s independence, daily living skills and community participation. Sophie benefits from structured intervention to build capacity, establish routines and reduce reliance on supports where possible.

Recommended areas of OT focus include:

* Development of structured daily routines to support sleep hygiene, meal preparation and household management  
* Capacity building for domestic skills, including meal planning and task sequencing  
* Support to develop community participation skills and confidence  
* Public transport training and graded exposure to supported travel where appropriate  
* Collaboration with support workers to ensure consistent implementation of strategies

Regular OT input is required to maintain functional gains, build independence and prevent functional decline.

**4\. Physiotherapy**

Physiotherapy intervention is recommended to address Sophie’s chronic back pain, limping gait pattern and reduced endurance, which negatively impact his functional capacity and participation in daily activities.

Recommended areas of focus include:

* Assessment and management of back pain and gait abnormalities  
* Development of a graded strengthening and conditioning program  
* Education in posture, pacing and safe movement strategies  
* Support to improve endurance and tolerance for standing and walking
