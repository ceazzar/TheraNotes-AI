## WHODAS 2.0 {#whodas-2.0}

The adult WHODAS 2.0 is a 36-item standardised measure assessing disability across six functional domains: understanding and communicating, getting around, self-care, getting along with people, life activities and participation in society. It is recognised by the NDIS as a best-practice tool for evaluating functional impairment related to psychosocial, cognitive and neurodevelopmental disability. Raw scores reflect the severity of functional impact, with higher scores indicating greater levels of disability.

Olivia’s WHODAS 2.0 results indicate a severe to extreme level of functional impairment across all domains, with particularly profound restrictions in communication, interpersonal functioning and community participation. Her scores are as follows:

* **Understanding and Communicating: 23/24 (96%)** This score reflects extreme impairment in Olivia’s ability to understand information, process instructions, retain information and communicate effectively. She experiences significant difficulty comprehending complex or multi-step information, following conversations and applying verbal guidance independently. These impairments are consistent with her Mild Intellectual Disability, mental health conditions and observed reliance on others for explanation, prompting and advocacy. As a result, Olivia is unable to independently manage appointments, follow medical advice or engage in problem-solving without substantial support.  
* **Getting Around: 17/20 (85%)** This score indicates severe impairment in mobility-related functioning. While Olivia is physically able to ambulate, chronic pain, stiffness, reduced endurance, poor motivation and emotional dysregulation significantly limit her functional mobility, particularly in community settings. She is unable to initiate or sustain independent community movement and relies on support workers for transport, pacing and engagement in all outdoor activities.  
* **Self-Care: 13/16 (81%)** Olivia’s score reflects severe impairment in self-care activities. She demonstrates inconsistent ability to complete personal hygiene, dressing and grooming tasks without prompting or assistance. Lower limb dressing is particularly compromised due to back pain and inability to bend safely. Cognitive and emotional factors further reduce her consistency, resulting in neglect of personal care routines without structured support.  
* **Getting Along with People: 20/20 (100%)** This maximum score represents extreme disability in interpersonal functioning. Olivia experiences significant difficulty regulating emotions, managing conflict, tolerating frustration and maintaining appropriate interactions with others. She demonstrates rigid behaviour patterns, frequent shouting, poor insight into the impact of her behaviour and limited capacity for compromise or de-escalation. These difficulties result in ongoing conflict within shared living environments and reliance on external supports to manage social interactions safely.  
* **Life Activities: 14/16 (88%)** This score indicates severe impairment in domestic and daily life activities. Olivia is unable to independently manage cooking, cleaning, laundry, health routines or household organisation due to a combination of physical limitations, poor task initiation, cognitive impairment and emotional dysregulation. Tasks requiring planning, sequencing or sustained effort are particularly compromised, resulting in dependence on support workers for daily functioning.  
* **Participation in Society: 32/32 (100%)** This maximum score reflects extreme restriction in Olivia’s ability to participate in community life. She is unable to independently access community environments, attend appointments, engage in social or recreational activities or sustain meaningful participation without full support. Barriers include emotional dysregulation, health instability, transport dependence, poor motivation and limited coping capacity. Without structured support, Olivia becomes socially isolated and disengaged from essential services.

Overall, Olivia’s WHODAS 2.0 profile demonstrates a very high level of disability, indicating severe to extreme functional impairment across all six domains, with a total score of 119/128 (93%) disability . Her highest levels of disability are evident in interpersonal functioning, participation in society and understanding and communicating, reflecting profound psychosocial and cognitive limitations.

These findings are consistent with observed functional presentation and strongly support the need for high-level, ongoing supports, including increased support worker hours, behaviour support intervention, allied health involvement and coordinated service delivery. Without sustained intervention, Olivia is at significant risk of further functional decline, health deterioration, housing instability and disengagement from essential services.

## Adult Sensory Profile {#adult-sensory-profile}

The Adult Sensory Profile (ASP) is a standardised self-report questionnaire used to evaluate an individual’s sensory processing patterns in everyday environments. It measures behavioural responses to sensory input across key sensory domains, including auditory, visual, tactile, movement and activity level, organised into four sensory processing quadrants: Low Registration, Sensation Seeking, Sensory Sensitivity and Sensation Avoiding. Scores are compared with same-aged peers, with results indicating whether an individual experiences sensory input similarly to others or demonstrates increased sensory seeking, sensitivity, avoidance or reduced sensory awareness. The ASP assists clinicians in identifying sensory modulation differences that may impact attention, emotional regulation, task engagement and participation in daily activities.

Olivia’s results on the Adult Sensory Profile revealed the following:

**Low Registration:** Much more than most people  
Olivia demonstrates significantly reduced awareness of sensory input, indicating a high neurological threshold for sensory stimuli. She frequently misses or is slow to respond to environmental cues, internal body signals and task-related prompts that others typically notice. This contributes to difficulty initiating tasks, reduced responsiveness to verbal instructions, inconsistent engagement in self-care routines and appearing disengaged or unmotivated. Olivia requires increased prompting, repetition and structured sensory cues to support task initiation and completion.

**Sensation Avoiding:** More than most people  
Olivia experiences environments as overwhelming and actively attempts to reduce sensory input to remain regulated. She demonstrates a strong preference for predictable, low-stimulation environments and becomes distressed in busy, noisy or unpredictable settings. This sensory avoidance contributes to withdrawal from community environments, resistance to appointments and outings, emotional escalation when demands are placed on her and rigid attempts to control her environment. Sensory overload frequently results in refusal behaviours and increased emotional dysregulation.

**Sensory Sensitivity:** Similar to most people  
Olivia’s sensory sensitivity scores fall within the typical range, indicating she does not consistently experience heightened reactivity to sensory input. However, when considered alongside her elevated Sensation Avoiding and Low Registration scores, this suggests that although she may not consciously identify sensory stimuli as distressing, her nervous system becomes overwhelmed, resulting in avoidance and withdrawal rather than overt sensory reactivity.

**Sensation Seeking:** Similar to most people  
Olivia does not actively seek additional sensory input to regulate her arousal levels. This contributes to low spontaneous engagement, limited self-initiation of activities and reliance on others to structure her day. She is unlikely to independently seek sensory input to increase alertness or motivation, further impacting task initiation and follow-through.

Olivia’s sensory profile demonstrates a dual pattern of Low Registration and Sensation Avoiding, indicating that she both misses important sensory cues and becomes overwhelmed by environmental demands. This combination significantly impacts her ability to initiate tasks, sustain attention, regulate emotions and participate consistently in daily and community activities. She may appear disengaged or resistant when sensory input is insufficient, yet become emotionally dysregulated and withdrawn when sensory demands increase.

Olivia’s ability to remain regulated and function effectively is dependent on predictable routines, structured environments, reduced sensory load, graded exposure to community settings and consistent external prompting. These sensory processing differences directly contribute to her difficulties with self-care consistency, community participation, emotional regulation and reliance on support workers, and strongly support the need for ongoing occupational therapy and structured daily supports.

**3\. Psychology**

Recommended areas of focus include:

* Support to manage anxiety, distress and frustration associated with perceived loss of control  
* Psychoeducation around emotional responses, routines and change tolerance
