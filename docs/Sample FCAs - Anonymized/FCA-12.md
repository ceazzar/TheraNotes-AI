## WHODAS 2.0  {#whodas-2.0}

The adult version of the WHODAS 2.0 is a 36-item measure that assesses disability in adults aged 18 years and older. It evaluates disability across six domains which include understanding and communicating, getting around, self-care, getting along with people, life activities and participation in society. The WHODAS 2.0 is recognised by the NDIS as a best-practice assessment for individuals with a primary disability. 

The outcome of the WHODAS 2.0 provides a percentage of impairment based on the results of the questions answered, with 100% indicating the highest level of identified disability.

* **Understanding and Communicating:** Mei scored 14/24 (58%) disability in this subdomain, which assesses the ability to understand information and communicate effectively. This reflects moderate to severe difficulty processing, retaining, and responding to verbal information, particularly when fatigued or overstimulated. Mei reports frequent forgetfulness, losing her train of thought during conversations, and difficulty comprehending written material such as forms or official correspondence. These limitations are consistent with her noted cognitive changes, anxiety, and trauma-related attention difficulties, resulting in reduced communication efficiency and independence.  
* **Getting Around:** Mei scored 16/20 (80%) disability in this subdomain, which examines a person’s ability to move about their environment. This score reflects significant mobility restriction due to chronic pain, right shoulder injury, and left wrist instability. Mei ambulates with a four-wheeled walker within the home. She has experienced multiple falls within the past year and avoids uneven or crowded environments due to fear of falling and anxiety. These difficulties contribute to physical deconditioning and reliance on her partner for transport and mobility supervision.  
* **Self-Care:** Mei scored 10/16 (63%) disability in this subdomain, which evaluates a person’s ability to engage in hygiene and personal self-care. This indicates moderate impairment. She requires assistance or supervision for showering, uses a shower stool and handheld hose, and experiences difficulty with lower-body dressing due to reduced reach, balance, and pain. Continence management is ongoing, with daytime pad use and pull-ups overnight due to urinary urgency. Her capacity fluctuates daily, depending on fatigue, pain, and psychological distress, which frequently lead to avoidance or incomplete routines.  
* **Getting Along with People:** Mei scored 14/20 (70%) disability in this subdomain, which measures an individual’s ability to interact with others and maintain relationships. This score reflects notable interpersonal and social participation challenges, primarily driven by anxiety, sensory overstimulation, and reduced confidence following her accident. She avoids unpredictable or noisy environments, preferring structured and supportive settings such as the Community Support Services friendship group. While she maintains strong relationships with her partner and close friends, social withdrawal and low mood continue to limit her broader engagement.  
* **Life Activities:** Mei scored 15/16 (94%) disability in this subdomain, which measures a person’s ability to perform domestic and work-related tasks. This represents severe functional limitation. Mei is largely unable to complete household routines independently due to pain, fatigue, and cognitive slowing. She relies heavily on her partner for cooking, cleaning, laundry, and errands. She uses seated strategies when performing light kitchen tasks but experiences rapid exhaustion and requires frequent rest. Her prior work and community roles have ceased entirely due to physical and emotional fatigue, highlighting profound loss of functional independence.  
* **Participation in Society:** Mei scored 24/32 (75%) disability in this subdomain, which assesses an individual’s ability to participate in community, social, and civic activities. This reflects severe restriction in community engagement due to trauma-related anxiety, sensory sensitivities, and mobility limitations. She often avoids leaving home alone and requires her partner’s support for appointments and social outings. Fatigue, pain, and fear of overstimulation prevent consistent participation in previously meaningful roles and activities, contributing to emotional isolation and reduced quality of life.

The above assessment was completed by Mei, who scored 93 out of 128 (73%) overall on the WHODAS 2.0, placing her within the moderate-to-severe range of functional disability. These findings highlight Mei’s greatest barriers in life activities, getting around, and participation in society, with significant limitations also evident in communication and self-care. This profile is consistent with her clinical presentation and demonstrates the substantial and enduring impact of her physical injuries, PTSD, and chronic pain on her independence, safety, and community participation.

These impairments interfere with her ability to comprehend instructions, manage finances, and complete paperwork without support. She has difficulty sustaining focus and retaining new information, particularly when fatigued or anxious, and relies heavily on her partner and Community Support Services case worker for assistance with administrative and organisational tasks. In summary, Mei’s functional impairments are enduring, multifactorial, and significantly reduce her ability to perform personal, domestic, and community activities independently. The interaction between her physical injuries, cognitive fatigue, and mental health conditions creates a cycle of pain, anxiety, and deconditioning that limits her participation and heightens her reliance on informal support. Ongoing allied health intervention, assistive equipment, environmental modification and formal support services are essential to ensure her safety, maintain her wellbeing, and rebuild capacity for daily and community engagement.
