# TheraNotes-AI Strategy: Reframe & Ship

**Date:** 2026-05-09
**Status:** Active strategic decision
**Context:** Resumed work after ~7-day stall following PMF doubt + launch-confidence wobble

---

## The decision

**Stop framing TheraNotes as an AU NDIS-allied-health SaaS. Reframe it as an internal Flourish Health productivity tool. External SaaS becomes a year-2 optionality, not a year-1 goal.**

This is not a downgrade. It is a correct sizing of the asset to the founder's situation.

---

## Why the previous frame was wrong

Earlier framing (implicit in the codebase, README, design-spec): build a polished AI FCA generator, launch publicly, compete in the AU clinical-AI market.

Reasons that frame fails today:

1. **Direct competitor exists with 2-year head start.** Everbility (Angela Mariani, AU OT founder, A$50/user/mo, AWS Startups, SOC 2/HIPAA/APP) explicitly markets FCA generation. Same founder profile. Two years ahead. ([Everbility pricing](https://www.everbility.com/pricing))
2. **Adjacent giants are one product cycle away.** Lyrebird Health just signed a 400-clinic deal with Healthia (OT, physio, podiatry, hand therapy) and explicitly markets NDIS PDFs. Heidi Health integrates with Echidna for NDIS notes. Splose ($46M Series A, ~20K clinicians) will bundle "AI report generation" into base tier.
3. **Macro is contracting.** Thriving Kids program removes under-9s from NDIS from Oct 2026 — direct hit to paediatric OT/SP volume that you'd be selling into.
4. **Regulatory wind is in your face for "AI generates FCAs" framing.** [Health Services Daily, 2026](https://www.healthservicesdaily.com.au/the-ndis-fca-problem-nobody-wants-to-talk-about/43355) explicitly named AI as making FCA quality worse. NDIS Commission has already issued an AI position statement on behaviour support plans — a precedent likely to extend to FCAs in 12-24 months.
5. **TAM ceiling is small.** AU AI-allied-health category total: ~$15M ARR ceiling, split across Everbility, Lyrebird, Heidi, Splose AI, Scripto, PatientNotes. Realistic capture for #4 entrant with no funding: $120K–$480K ARR ceiling after 18-24 months of dedicated sales.

The instinct that triggered the stall was correct: chasing this as a venture-style SaaS while running Flourish full-time and three other AI projects is irrational. **The instinct was directionally right; the conclusion ("PMF doubt") was wrong.**

## Why the new frame is right

The previous frame ignored the actual asset.

| Frame | Goal | Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| **SaaS launch (rejected)** | $30K+ MRR competing with Everbility/Lyrebird/Splose | Lose the war, ship nothing, abandon |
| **Internal Flourish tool (chosen)** | Halve OT report-writing time across 3-5 OTs | $40-90K/yr recovered OT capacity, no payroll, no GTM |

### Internal-use ROI math (conservative)

Assume 4 Flourish OTs writing 3 FCAs/month each at 8h × $150/hr loaded cost:

- 12 FCAs/month × $1,200 = **~$14,400/month** of OT capacity in writing
- Halving this = **~$86K/yr** recovered as billable client capacity
- At 50% optimism: **~$40-50K/yr**, equivalent to a part-time hire with no overhead

This is a hire's worth of capacity. It pays for itself immediately and compounds with Flourish's growth. **External revenue is no longer a precondition for the project being worth doing.**

### What we keep optionality on

- If 6 months of internal use shows the output is consistently strong and OTs love it: alpha-share to 1-2 friendly NDIS practices in year 2.
- If by month 18 there's external pull: consider a productised SaaS with a **planner-validated wedge** ("FCAs that don't get rejected"), not a feature-comparison race against Everbility.
- Until then: zero pricing page, zero marketing site, zero outbound sales.

## The actual reason for the stall (with evidence)

The stated doubt was "PMF + ability to launch a product that works." The codebase audit reveals the doubt was anchored in a configuration bug, not a market signal:

- **All AI calls defaulted to `gpt-4o`**, not the `gpt-5.4`/`gpt-5.4-pro` family the design-spec called for. Now updated to `gpt-5.5`.
- **`queryRag` swallowed all errors** via `try { ... } catch { return { foundational: [], userStyle: [] } }`. If anything in the embedding or Supabase call failed (RLS misconfig, RPC missing, network blip), reports were generated with **zero exemplar context** and the dev would never know. Now throws.
- **`createServiceClient` used `!` non-null assertions** that let a missing `SUPABASE_SERVICE_ROLE_KEY` flow through to Supabase, producing silent auth failures rather than a clear "key missing" error. Now fails fast with a named error.

Every test the founder ran was likely judging the *idea* against output produced by:
- The wrong model
- Empty RAG (no exemplars, no clinical voice)
- Possibly broken auth on RAG queries

The output was, predictably, generic. The founder rationalised this as "PMF doubt" rather than seeing it as broken infra. This is a normal cognitive failure pattern: when the system underperforms, blame the strategy before checking the wires.

**Fixing the wires is the precondition for any honest PMF read.**

## Session log: what shipped on 2026-05-09

This was one continuous session. The session went from "system is broken in ways the founder couldn't see" to "system produces planner-grade FCAs from structured intake."

### Phase 0 → Phase A → prompt tuning → Phase B (chronological)

**Phase 0 — bug discovery & verification.** Ran the existing E2E test on a fresh dev server. Surfaced four compounded failures that had been hiding behind each other and producing the "PMF doubt" feeling:
1. `gpt-4o` defaults across all generation paths instead of the `gpt-5.x` family the design-spec called for.
2. `queryRag` was silently swallowing all Supabase errors and returning empty exemplars — every test had been judging output produced with **zero exemplar context**.
3. `SUPABASE_SERVICE_ROLE_KEY` was literally an empty-string in `.env.local` (`""`), so RAG queries were silently auth-failing on top of the swallow.
4. `OPENAI_API_KEY` was an OpenRouter key (`sk-or-v1...`) overriding `.env.local` from shell environment.

Fixed all four. Service role key found in Keychain mismatch (different project), pasted from Supabase dashboard. OpenAI key swapped to project key from Keychain. Removed temperature handling for gpt-5.5 reasoning model (only default supported). RAG now surfaces errors loudly. `createServiceClient` throws named errors when env missing.

**Phase A — Responses API migration.** Ported `generate`, `revise` (routing + revision), `refine` from `client.chat.completions.create` to `client.responses.create` with `reasoning.effort=high`. Updated token accounting to `input_tokens` / `output_tokens` (output includes reasoning). Re-ran E2E. Validated that Phase A delivered the reasoning-depth gains the founder's instinct had predicted: Part E goal fabrication eliminated, proportionality-boundary phrases appearing, deeper clinical INSUF flagging. Latency +27%, cost ~3x — but actual measurement put it at $0.25–1.74 per FCA, not the $15–35 originally feared.

**30-min prompt-tuning pass.** Added required `Emotional Regulation` subsection rule to Part B template. Strengthened the anti-repetition rule in `prompts.ts` to forbid any single advocacy phrase appearing more than once per section. Re-ran E2E: dedicated Emotional Regulation subsection appeared with proportionality framing nearly verbatim from the gold standard ("regulation pattern is primarily one of avoidance and withdrawal rather than acute behavioural escalation… should not be overstated as behaviourally disruptive").

**Phase B — staged-generation intake.** The big architectural shift. Reframed from "generate everything, hide gaps with INSUF flags" to "generate only what's data-supported, mark the rest as pending."

What shipped:
- New migration `007_intake_fields.sql`: adds `intake_metadata JSONB` and `ndis_goals TEXT[]` to `assessments` (applied 2026-05-09).
- `requires` and `references` declarations on every section in `template.json`.
- New `lib/ai/intake.ts`: bucket-availability checker (`client`, `assessor`, `assessment`, `clinical_notes`, `standardized_scores`, `ndis_goals`, `report_so_far`).
- Skip-pending gate at the top of `generateSection` — no LLM/RAG work happens for sections whose `requires` aren't satisfied. Returns `{ status: 'pending', missing: [...] }` with zero token spend.
- New `lib/ai/header.ts`: deterministic markdown-table builder for the Header section. No LLM call. Latency went from ~15s to **0.9s**.
- Structured intake injected into Part D prompt (WHODAS + Sensory Profile interpretation) and Part E prompt (NDIS goals quoted verbatim, no extrapolation).
- `/generate` page extended with 5 collapsible intake sections (Client Details, Assessor & Assessment, WHODAS Scores, Sensory Profile, NDIS Goals). Section preview banner: "Will generate X of 8 sections."
- `FormattedReport` renders pending sections as dashed-border placeholder cards explaining what intake to add.
- API route returns `status` + `missing` per section; persisted in report record.
- E2E test populated with full intake from the Peter Parker gold-standard reference data.
